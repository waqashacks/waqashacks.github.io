About Me

Hi, I'm Waqas Ibrahim — a cybersecurity enthusiast who went from flashing phone firmware as a teenager to building enterprise SOC labs. Yes, I was that kid who bricked their phone multiple times and somehow always managed to fix it (eventually).

How It All Started

My journey into cybersecurity didn't start with a dramatic hacking moment from a movie. It started with curiosity and a lot of broken gadgets.

In my younger days, I had a passion for IT that bordered on obsession. I spent hours tinkering with phone firmware, trying to understand how these little computers in our pockets actually worked. I loved breaking things and figuring out how to fix them — a trait that still serves me well in cybersecurity.

What really fascinated me was how computers talk to each other. The idea that packets of data could travel across networks, find their destination, and deliver messages felt like magic. I wanted to understand the magic.

Fun fact: My first ever program was written in Java. It was probably terrible code, but it worked, and I was hooked.

The Path to Cybersecurity

After finishing my computing and engineering studies at DMUIC, I had many career paths to choose from. But I kept gravitating toward one question: "How do I break this, and how do I protect it?"

That question led me to pursue a BSc (Hons) in Cyber Security at De Montfort University, where I graduated with a 2:1. During my degree, I explored various domains — from penetration testing to network security — but I found myself consistently drawn to the defensive side of security.

Digital Forensics & Incident Response (DFIR) and Security Operations (SOC) became my primary interests. There's something deeply satisfying about investigating incidents, analyzing logs, and piecing together what happened during a security event. It's like being a digital detective.

What I Do

I'm a cybersecurity graduate with hands-on experience across penetration testing, SOC operations, and digital forensics. My focus is on the defensive side — detecting, analyzing, and responding to threats.

Home SOC & Lab Projects

I don't just read about security — I build it. I actively create realistic enterprise-style labs to understand how security tools work in real environments.

My home lab includes:

  • Active Directory Domain Controller with domain-joined endpoints
  • pfSense firewall for network segmentation and traffic control
  • Wazuh SIEM with deployed agents for centralized logging
  • Windows and Linux endpoints simulating enterprise environments

This portfolio documents these labs step by step, focusing on architecture, detection workflows, and the lessons I learn along the way. Because the best way to understand SOC operations is to actually build and operate one.
